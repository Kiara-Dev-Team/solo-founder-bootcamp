# Application Guide

Everything you need to know about applying to Y Combinator.

## How to Apply

### Application Timeline

YC runs two batches per year:

- **Winter Batch** - Applications due in September, program runs January-March
- **Summer Batch** - Applications due in March, program runs June-August

Timeline:
1. **Application submission** - Online form + video
2. **Review period** - 2-3 weeks
3. **Interviews** - 10-minute interviews (virtual or in-person)
4. **Decisions** - Sent within a few days of interviews
5. **Batch starts** - ~2 months after acceptance

### Application Requirements

#### 1. Written Application

Complete the online form at [ycombinator.com/apply](https://www.ycombinator.com/apply)

Key sections:
- **Company description** - One-line description
- **What you're building** - Detailed explanation
- **Problem you're solving** - Why does this matter?
- **Your solution** - How does it work?
- **Progress/traction** - Metrics, users, revenue
- **Founders** - Background and relationship
- **Market** - Size and opportunity
- **Competitive advantage** - What makes you special?

#### 2. Application Video

Requirements:
- **Length:** 1 minute max
- **Format:** YouTube, Vimeo, or similar
- **Content:** All founders introducing themselves and the idea
- **Quality:** Good audio is more important than video

Tips:
- ‚úÖ Be natural and conversational
- ‚úÖ Show, don't just tell (demo if possible)
- ‚úÖ Explain the problem clearly
- ‚úÖ Keep it simple and focused
- ‚ùå Don't use fancy editing or effects
- ‚ùå Don't read from a script robotically

### Eligibility

You can apply if:
- ‚úÖ You have at least one co-founder
- ‚úÖ At least one founder can relocate to San Francisco for the batch
- ‚úÖ You're working on your startup full-time (or will be)
- ‚úÖ You have less than $10M in total funding (for YC's standard program)

You don't need:
- ‚ùå A finished product
- ‚ùå Revenue or users
- ‚ùå A company incorporated
- ‚ùå To be based in the US

## Tips and Advice

### From YC Partners

#### What YC Looks For

**1. Founders**
- Determination and resilience
- Ability to build and ship
- Domain expertise
- Strong relationship between co-founders
- Communication skills

**2. Idea**
- Solving a real problem
- Clear value proposition
- Potential for significant growth
- Good market timing
- Feasibility

**3. Progress**
- Evidence of execution
- User feedback
- Growth trajectory
- Learning and iteration

#### Application Tips

**Be Clear and Concise**
```
‚ùå Bad: "We're building an AI-powered blockchain platform that leverages 
machine learning to disrupt the paradigm of traditional systems..."

‚úÖ Good: "We make it easy for restaurants to accept online orders 
without paying high commission fees."
```

**Show, Don't Tell**
```
‚ùå Bad: "We're going to be huge and disrupt the industry."

‚úÖ Good: "We have 1,000 users who have processed $50K in transactions 
with 40% month-over-month growth."
```

**Be Specific About the Problem**
```
‚ùå Bad: "Scheduling is hard."

‚úÖ Good: "Sales teams waste 2 hours per day scheduling meetings because 
they have to check multiple calendars and send dozens of emails back and forth."
```

**Know Your Numbers**
```
‚úÖ Revenue: $10K/month, growing 20% MoM
‚úÖ Users: 2,000 active users, 15% retained after 30 days
‚úÖ Unit economics: $50 CAC, $200 LTV
```

### Interview Preparation

#### The YC Interview

Format:
- **Duration:** 10 minutes
- **Setting:** Virtual (video call) or in-person
- **Attendees:** 2-3 YC partners
- **Style:** Rapid-fire questions

What to expect:
- Questions come fast
- Partners may seem skeptical (it's normal)
- They're testing how you think and respond
- Be ready to pivot the conversation

#### Common Questions

**About the Product:**
- What do you do?
- Who are your users?
- How do they use it currently?
- What problem does this solve?
- Show me a demo
- What's your distribution strategy?

**About Traction:**
- How many users do you have?
- What's your revenue?
- What's your growth rate?
- What's your retention?
- What are your unit economics?

**About Market:**
- How big is the market?
- Who are your competitors?
- Why now?
- What's your unfair advantage?

**About Team:**
- How did you meet?
- What makes you uniquely qualified?
- What have you built together?
- What happens if a co-founder quits?

**Hypotheticals:**
- What if [competitor] does this?
- What would you do with $1M?
- What's the worst thing about your idea?
- Why will you succeed when others have failed?

#### Interview Tips

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Practice with a timer (10 minutes goes fast)
- ‚úÖ Know your metrics cold
- ‚úÖ Be honest about challenges
- ‚úÖ Listen carefully to questions
- ‚úÖ Be specific in your answers
- ‚úÖ Show passion and conviction
- ‚úÖ Be open to feedback

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Give long, rambling answers
- ‚ùå Be defensive about criticism
- ‚ùå Make up numbers you don't know
- ‚ùå Talk over the partners
- ‚ùå Avoid difficult questions
- ‚ùå Be arrogant or dismissive

### Example Scripts

**Opening (30 seconds):**
"We're [Company Name]. We help [target customer] do [specific thing]. 

Right now they use [current solution], which has [specific problems]. 

We built [your solution] which [specific benefit]. 

We have [traction metrics] and are growing [growth rate]."

**Demo (if asked):**
"Let me show you. [Customer] needs to [task]. Here's how they do it with us..."

Keep it simple, fast, and focused on value.

## Common Mistakes

### Application Mistakes

**1. Unclear Description**
- Being too vague about what you do
- Using buzzwords instead of clear language
- Not explaining the problem well

**2. Weak Evidence**
- No traction or proof points
- Vague metrics or claims
- Not showing real user feedback

**3. Team Issues**
- Missing co-founder context
- Unclear equity split
- No evidence of ability to execute

**4. Poor Video**
- Reading from a script
- Inaudible audio
- Too long or too short
- Not showing all founders

### Interview Mistakes

**1. Not Knowing Numbers**
```
Partner: "What's your revenue?"
You: "Um, I'm not sure exactly... maybe around..."

‚ùå This is a red flag. Know your metrics!
```

**2. Being Too Defensive**
```
Partner: "Why would anyone use this?"
You: "That's a great question and you clearly don't understand..."

‚ùå Don't be defensive. Listen and respond thoughtfully.
```

**3. Long Answers**
```
Partner: "What do you do?"
You: "Well, it all started when I was working at..."

‚ùå Get to the point quickly. You have 10 minutes.
```

**4. Not Listening**
```
Partner: "How do you make money?"
You: "Our growth strategy is..."

‚ùå Answer the question asked. Don't pivot to what you want to say.
```

### Strategy Mistakes

**Waiting Too Long to Apply**
- Apply early, even if you're not "ready"
- Getting rejected is fine - you can reapply
- The application process itself is valuable

**Overthinking the Application**
- Don't spend months perfecting it
- Clear and honest > polished and vague
- Launch your product instead of endlessly refining your app

**Applying Without a Co-founder**
- YC strongly prefers teams
- Solo founders have much lower acceptance rates
- Find a co-founder before applying

## After Applying

### If You Get In

üéâ Congratulations! Here's what happens:

1. **Accept the offer** - You have a few days to decide
2. **Sign documents** - Standard YC agreement
3. **Prepare to relocate** - At least one founder needs to be in SF
4. **Join the batch** - Meet other founders, weekly dinners, office hours
5. **Build and grow** - Intense 3 months of growth
6. **Demo Day** - Present to investors

### If You Don't Get In

Don't worry! Many successful companies were rejected multiple times:

- **Reapply** - You can apply every batch
- **Build traction** - Use feedback to improve
- **Learn** - The process itself is educational
- **Keep going** - Most unicorns weren't in YC

Famous rejections:
- Airbnb was rejected once before being accepted
- Many successful non-YC companies exist
- Getting rejected doesn't mean your idea is bad

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a finished product?**
A: No, but having something users can interact with helps.

**Q: How important is traction?**
A: Very important, but early traction counts (even 10 users who love you).

**Q: Can I apply without a co-founder?**
A: Yes, but your chances are much lower. YC strongly prefers teams.

**Q: What if we're not technical?**
A: You need at least one technical founder who can build the product.

**Q: Can we apply from outside the US?**
A: Yes! YC accepts international companies.

**Q: Do we need to be incorporated?**
A: No, YC can help with that if you're accepted.

**Q: What's the acceptance rate?**
A: Around 1.5%, but don't let that discourage you!
